Introduction

CRISP-DM (Cross-Industry Standard Process for Data Mining) is a widely used data science methodology that provides a structured approach for planning and executing data mining projects. It helps organizations turn raw data into meaningful insights to support business decisions.

The methodology is flexible and can be applied across industries such as retail, finance, healthcare, and marketing. CRISP-DM consists of six phases—Business Understanding, Data Understanding, Data Preparation, Modeling, Evaluation, and Deployment—that guide the entire process from identifying problems to delivering actionable results.

Its systematic and iterative approach ensures that data projects remain aligned with business goals and maintain high quality throughout.

Benefits of CRISP-DM

Helps Teams Collaborate:

Provides a clear roadmap so business users and data scientists can work together efficiently.

Reduces Project Risk:

Ensures projects stay aligned with business objectives and data quality standards.

Improves Decision-Making:

Translates raw data into actionable insights that guide strategic decisions.

Supports Repeatable Process:

Can be applied to multiple projects and industries consistently.
